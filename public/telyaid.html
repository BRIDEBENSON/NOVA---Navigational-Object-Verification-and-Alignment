Celestial Object Visibility Checker

How to Use the Star Visibility Checker

  1. Choose a celestial object from the dropdown menu or enter custom coordinates (RA/Dec).
  2. Select your location from the predefined options or allow the system to detect your current location.
  3. If using a custom location, enter your latitude and longitude in decimal degrees.
  4. Click "Check Visibility" to see if the object is currently visible from your location.
  5. The results will display the object's current altitude and azimuth, along with rise/set times.
Note: For best results, ensure your location data is accurate. Negative values are used for South latitude and West longitude.
